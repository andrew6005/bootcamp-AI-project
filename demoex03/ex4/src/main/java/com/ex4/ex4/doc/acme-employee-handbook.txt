ACME Corporation — Employee Handbook (2024 Edition)

=== CHAPTER 1: WORKING HOURS AND LEAVE POLICY ===

Standard working hours are Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Employees may flex their start time between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM provided they complete 8 hours per day.

Annual Leave
Full-time employees receive 15 days of annual leave per year. Leave accrues at 1.25 days per month. Unused leave may be carried over up to a maximum of 5 days into the next calendar year.

Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical certificate is required for absences of 3 or more consecutive days.

Public Holidays
ACME observes all gazetted public holidays. If a public holiday falls on a weekend, the following Monday is taken as a substitute.

=== CHAPTER 2: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS ===

Salary Review
Performance reviews and salary adjustments occur annually in March. Promotions may result in an immediate salary adjustment outside this cycle.

Health Insurance
All full-time employees and their immediate dependents are covered under ACME's group health insurance plan. Coverage includes inpatient, outpatient, dental, and optical benefits. The plan provider is MediShield Pro.

Retirement Plan
ACME contributes 5% of the employee's monthly salary to their retirement fund, in addition to the mandatory statutory contribution.

=== CHAPTER 3: REMOTE WORK POLICY ===

Hybrid Work
Employees may work remotely up to 2 days per week, subject to manager approval. New employees (within their first 3 months) are expected to work on-site full-time.

Equipment
ACME provides a laptop and necessary peripherals for remote work. Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe and productive home workspace.

Data Security
When working remotely, employees must use the company VPN. Confidential documents must not be downloaded to personal devices.

=== CHAPTER 4: CODE OF CONDUCT ===

Respect and Inclusion
ACME is committed to a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and bullying. All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect regardless of their background, identity, or role.

Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside employment or business interests that may conflict with ACME's interests to their manager and HR.

Confidentiality
Employees must not disclose proprietary information, client data, or trade secrets during or after employment.

=== CHAPTER 5: IT AND ACCEPTABLE USE ===

Company Systems
Company devices and systems are for business use. Limited personal use is acceptable provided it does not interfere with work or violate this policy.

Prohibited Activities
- Installing unlicensed software
- Accessing inappropriate or illegal content
- Sharing login credentials
- Disabling security software

Password Policy
Passwords must be at least 12 characters and changed every 90 days. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all company accounts.

=== CHAPTER 6: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT ===

Goal Setting
At the start of each year, employees set SMART goals in collaboration with their manager using the ACME Perform portal.

Mid-Year Check-in
A formal mid-year check-in occurs in July to assess progress and adjust goals if needed.

Annual Review
The annual performance review takes place in February. Ratings are: Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement.

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Employees rated "Needs Improvement" for two consecutive cycles will be placed on a PIP. The PIP outlines specific targets and a 90-day improvement window.

=== CHAPTER 7: LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT ===

Training Budget
Each employee receives an annual training budget of $1,500 for courses, conferences, and certifications relevant to their role.

Internal Learning
ACME's internal LMS (Learning Management System) offers over 300 e-learning courses. Completion of mandatory compliance training is tracked quarterly.

Mentorship Program
A voluntary mentorship program pairs junior employees with senior leaders for 6-month engagements.

=== CHAPTER 8: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES ===

Informal Warning
For minor infractions, a manager may issue an informal verbal warning documented in the employee file.

Formal Warning
Repeated or serious infractions result in a formal written warning. Two formal warnings within 12 months may lead to termination.

Summary Dismissal
Gross misconduct (theft, fraud, violence, serious data breach) may result in immediate dismissal without prior warning.

=== CHAPTER 9: OFFBOARDING ===

Notice Period
The standard notice period is 1 month. Senior roles (Manager and above) require 2 months' notice.

Knowledge Transfer
Departing employees must complete a knowledge transfer checklist and hand over ongoing projects to their manager or designated colleague.

Exit Interview
HR conducts a voluntary exit interview to gather feedback. Responses are kept confidential and used to improve the employee experience.

Return of Assets
All company equipment, access cards, and materials must be returned on the last day of employment.
